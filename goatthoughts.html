Overview

Goat Thoughts is a tool that allows people attending therapy, doctors, or other types of appointments to log and organize their thoughts and concerns inbetween sessions.

Simply add a provider, add an upcoming session, and start logging your Goat Thoughts for the next time you see your provider.

The Problem

Talking to professionals is intimidating for many people. Those who attend therapy appointments often forget what they want to talk to their provider about by the time the appointment starts

An opportunity to improve therapy

If your mind goes blank when you show up to your doctors' office, don't worry, you're not alone. When there's so much to get to, how do you know what to prioritize?

What if there was a way for you to prepare for your appointment beforehand, with little effort on your end?

The Solution

Goat Thoughts is an Android app that helps clients achieve their goals in therapy by logging and organizing their thoughts and emotions in between sessions.
Using the principles of cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), Goat Thoughts creates a beneficial experience for both clients and providers.

The Process

Ideation

Goat Thoughts is a solution not just for clients, but therapists and healthcare facilities as well. While ideating on Goat Thoughts, I talked to patients, clients, and providers at a variety of mental health facilities and outpatient offices to hear more about their experiences with therapy and what would actually be useful for getting the most out of a session.

User testing

Using mockups designed in Figma, users tested four main flows (Adding a provider, adding a session, adding a goat thought, and accessing existing goat thoughts)

Users included:

  • Clients with eating, mood, and/or trauma disorders admitted in Partial Hospitalization Programs (PHP) and Intensive Outpatient Programs (IOP)
  • Outpatient therapy clients
  • Outpatient, IOP, and PHP therapists

Reiterating

What users had to say:

  • Users wanted a way to log and identify multiple types of providers (dieticians, psychiatrists, etc)
  • Therapists suggested putting a character limit on thoughts, to encourage concise thoughts
  • Clients wanted a calendar view to log sessions

In addressing their feedback, all recommended changes were made in the new designs
